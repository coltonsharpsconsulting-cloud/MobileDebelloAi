🤙 Hollie's Epic Moment — She Will Brag About This
  • Guided Snorkel Tour at Shaw's Cove — OC
    Ocean Institute runs guided snorkel tours directly at Shaw's Cove. All gear provided, ages 6+. Calm protected cove with 15–30ft visibility. Hollie comes home and tells every friend. Book ahead — summer fills fast.
    🔗 oceaninstitute.org — search guided snorkel tours
  • Little Treasure Cove Sea Caves — Crystal Cove
    Accessible only at super low (negative) tide — mornings in summer. Sea caves, not a beach. You find it, you feel like you discovered something. That's the story she tells.
    Check tide charts at crystalcovestatepark.org before you go
  • ATV on Oceano Dunes — Pismo
    Only beach in California where you can ride ATVs on the actual sand. Rent on-site. If you go Pismo — this is Hollie's moment, no question.
🌿 Sondra's Must-Haves
  • SLO Thursday Night Farmers Market — Pismo area
    Every Thursday, 6pm on Higuera Street in downtown SLO. 100+ vendors, live music, street performers, local food. This is the one. If you're going Pismo and the dates line up — this is Sondra's night.
  • Laguna Beach Saturday Farmers Market — OC
    Saturdays 8am–noon at Forest Rd & 3rd St. 40+ vendors, artisan foods, local crafts. Small and intimate. OC's version of Sondra's morning.
  • Laguna Beach Sawdust Art Festival — OC
    Summer-only. Local Laguna artists only — no outside vendors allowed. Handmade everything. This is the hippie artisan experience in OC. Check dates when you lock the trip window.
  • Gaia's Garden & Apothecary — Morro Bay (near Pismo)
    Voted best crystal and rock shop in the area. Crystals, herbs, community readings. Morro Bay is 20 min north of Pismo — worth the detour for Sondra.
  • Avila Beach Friday Market — Pismo area
    April–September, Fridays 4–8pm on the Avila Beach promenade. Live music, local vendors, right on the water. Slow evening, Sondra's pace exactly.
  • Sunrise at the beach — either destination
    Coffee, no agenda, watch the sun come up. This one costs nothing and fills her up the most. Build it in as a non-negotiable morning.
🍃 Adults Evening — Cannabis + Night Out
  • Catalyst Cannabis — Grover Beach (Pismo area)
    5 minutes from Pismo. Serves Pismo Beach, Arroyo Grande, Shell Beach. Top-rated budtenders, full menu. Best option for the Pismo route. Order online for pickup or delivery to the rental.
    catalyst-cannabis.com — Grover Beach location
  • Natural Healing Center / SLO Cal Roots — SLO
    Two well-rated dispensaries in San Luis Obispo, 12 min from Pismo. SLO County Visitors Guide lists the full directory at slovisitorsguide.com.
  • Koastal Kush Delivery — OC Route
    San Clemente has no dispensaries in city limits — but Koastal Kush delivers directly to San Clemente, Laguna Beach, Dana Point. Order to the hotel. Pair it with an evening at The Cellar or San Clemente Wine Company after the kids are down.
    koastalkushoc.com
  • The Cellar — San Clemente (OC)
    Wine bar + restaurant + cheese shop on Avenida Del Mar. Live music on weekends. Local crowd. The adult evening in OC — delivery arrives, you walk to The Cellar. That's the move.
  • Puffers of Pismo — Pismo
    Tiny speakeasy. Small plates, cheese boards, great wine list. Adult night out in Pismo — stop at Catalyst first, end at Puffers.
🤿 Snorkeling & Tide Pools
  • Shaw's Cove — Laguna Beach
    Protected cove, 15–30ft visibility, calm water. Best snorkel spot in OC. Gear rental from Laguna Surf & Sport nearby. Guided tours via Ocean Institute.
  • Crystal Cove Tide Pool Areas
    4 areas: Reef Point, Rocky Bight, Pelican Point, Treasure Cove. Protected state park — life actually lives here. Best at morning low tides in summer.
  • Treasure Island — Laguna
    Turquoise water, rocks to snorkel around, coves to explore. Public beach at the base of the Montage Resort. Bring your own gear or rent from Laguna Surf & Sport.
  • Shell Beach — Pismo area
    Quiet cove just north of Pismo. Good visibility when the water is calm. Less known, less crowded than the main beach snorkel spots.
🥾 Mellow Scenic (Not a Workout)
  • Crystal Cove Beach Trail — OC
    Easy flat trail along the bluff and beach inside Crystal Cove State Park. Coves, views, tide pools along the way. Everyone can do it, no gear needed.
  • Oso Flaco Lake Boardwalk — Pismo
    2.2 miles, flat, boardwalk the whole way. Crosses a lake, through dunes, ends at the ocean. Birding, peaceful, stunning. Sondra's trail if you go Pismo.
  • Pismo Preserve Bluff Trail
    Easy bluff walk with panoramic Pacific views. Watch for dolphins from the trail. E-bikes available to rent. Sondra, Nikki, easy win for the whole group.
🛍️ Shopping — Local & Indie Only
  • Laguna Beach Downtown Boutiques — OC
    Ocean Ave and Forest Ave are lined with independent galleries, clothing, jewelry, art. No chain stores allowed in Laguna's downtown. This is the local shopping experience.
  • Sawdust Art Festival — Laguna Beach
    Summer-only festival of local artists. Handmade jewelry, ceramics, sculpture, clothing. Sondra shops, kids get a souvenir they actually picked, Nikki finds something for the house.
  • Avenida Del Mar — San Clemente
    SC's main street. Local surf shops, small boutiques, not a tourist trap. Walk it after dinner.
  • Downtown SLO — Higuera Street
    SLO's main drag. Indie shops, local clothing, bookstores, cafes. Best combined with the Thursday Night Farmers Market — shop the street, then hit the market.

The Destination
Orange County Coast
~5.5 hrs from Peoria · Crystal Cove · Laguna Beach · San Clemente
Shorter Drive Tide Pools Surf Town Vibe

Closest option. Crystal Cove is protected state park land — completely different from commercial beaches. San Clemente is a mellow surf town, not a resort scene. Laguna has coves and a real local art community.

Where Locals Actually Go
  • Little Treasure Cove Sea Caves — Crystal Cove
    Only reachable at super low tide. Sea caves — kids go wild. Check tide charts before you go. Negative low tides only, usually mornings in summer.
  • Shaw's Cove — Laguna Beach
    Best tidepooling in Laguna. 58 steps down from Fairview St, north of downtown. Inside the marine reserve. Not on tourist maps.
  • Table Rock Beach — South Laguna
    Off Bluff Drive via Table Rock Drive. Quiet, no crowds. Good for just sitting. Skip the main Laguna beach scene entirely.
  • Lost Winds Beach — San Clemente
    Bluffs of south SC. Trail between homes at 2004 Calle De Los Alamos — drops past railroad tracks to sand. No signs, no parking lot, no crowd.
  • Treasure Island — Laguna (below the Montage)
    Turquoise water, hidden coves, snorkeling rocks. Beach is public even though it's at the base of a fancy resort. Great for the kids to explore.
  • Crystal Cove Tide Pool Areas
    4 spots inside the park: Reef Point, Rocky Bight, Pelican Point, Treasure Cove. Protected land — pools are genuinely full of life. Best at morning low tides.
Where Locals Eat — San Clemente & Laguna
  • Avila's El Ranchito
    Del Mar Ave staple. Family-run for years. Tacos, enchiladas, margaritas. The real deal — not a chain, not tourist-priced.
    ✓ Kids eat well here
  • Cafe Mimosa
    French-inspired breakfast, family-owned since 2008. Locals start their mornings here. Avenida Del Mar.
    ✓ Breakfast portions — good for small eaters
  • Antoine's Café
    Downtown SC institution. The kind of place regulars come back to. Easy, unfussy, solid food.
  • The Cellar
    Avenida Del Mar. Wine bar + restaurant + cheese shop in one. New American, flatbreads, live music on weekends. Feels like a secret.
  • San Clemente Wine Company
    Del Mar. Rotating small plates, great wine selection. Local crowd, zero tourist energy. Adult evening move.
The Destination
Pismo Beach + San Luis Obispo
~7.5 hrs from Peoria · Central Coast · Quietest Option
Most Peaceful Walkable Beach Unique Activities Longer Drive

Least touristy of all the options. Pismo has a walkable downtown and beach with no resort scene. SLO is a college town 12 miles inland — charming and affordable. The dunes and coves here are unique to California.

Where Locals Actually Go
  • Wilmar Stairs — North End of Pismo
    Hidden stair access to the quiet north beach. Walk further north and you hit a white-walled cove only reachable at low tide. Almost nobody's there.
  • Oso Flaco Lake Trail
    Southern Pismo State Park. 2.2 miles — boardwalk past a lake, through dunes, out to the ocean. Birding, completely peaceful, totally off the tourist radar. Good for all ages.
  • Dinosaur Caves Park
    Free coastal park. Giant dinosaur sculptures and dino eggs to climb, bluff views over the ocean. Locals bring their kids here — it's not in the travel guides. Abi, Hollie, Jameson and Sondra will love it.
  • Avila Barn
    Mini-donkeys, goats, heritage chickens. Local produce, snack shack, ice cream. Kids do the animals, adults browse the farm store. Slow and easy.
  • Pismo Preserve
    Hiking trails with sweeping Pacific views. E-bike rentals available. Watch for dolphins from the bluff trail. No crowds, genuinely gorgeous.
  • Clam Digging on the Beach
    Pismo is literally named for Pismo clams. Grab a license, rent a bucket, dig. The kids dig them up, you cook them. It's a whole activity and a free dinner in one.
  • Oceano Dunes SVRA
    Only beach in California where you can drive or ride ATVs on the sand. Rent ATVs on-site. Completely different energy from a regular beach day — the kids will talk about it for years.
Where Locals Eat — Pismo Beach
  • Splash Cafe
    Clam chowder institution since 1989. No-frills, locals line up here. Chowder bread bowl is the move. Simple and perfect.
    ✓ Kids do well — simple comfort food
  • Beachin' Biscuits
    Family-owned. From-scratch biscuits & gravy, burritos, sandwiches. Short walk from the beach. The kind of breakfast spot you come back to every morning.
    ✓ Easy to ask for half portions
  • Brad's Restaurant
    Short walk from the beach. Laid-back, welcoming. The tri-tip sandwich is a local legend. No fuss, no tourist markup.
  • Cracked Crab
    Locals' seafood pick. Fresh local catch. They dump buckets of crab on the table — messy, fun, memorable. The kind of dinner the whole trip gets remembered by.
    ✓ Fun for kids — the dump-bucket experience
  • Puffers of Pismo
    Tiny speakeasy vibe. Small plates, cheese boards, amazing wine list. The adult night out after the kids are down.
  • Penny's Diner
    Classic American diner packed with locals. Big breakfast plates. Dave's Mess — eggs, home fries, bacon, ham, sausage, cheddar — feeds the whole table.
    ✓ Kids eat half a plate and are done
